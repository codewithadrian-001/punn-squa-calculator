Understanding Genetics & Inheritance

What is a Punnett Square?

A Punnett square is a diagram used to predict the genotypes of offspring from a cross between two parents. It shows all possible combinations of alleles (gene variants) that could be inherited.

Dominant vs. Recessive Traits

Dominant: A trait that appears in the offspring even if inherited from just one parent.

Recessive: A trait that only appears when inherited from both parents.

Hair Color Inheritance

Hair color is controlled by the MC1R gene and other genes that produce melanin. Black and brown hair are dominant over blonde hair.

Eye Color Inheritance

The OCA2 gene is primarily responsible for eye color. Brown eyes are dominant over blue eyes.

Skin Tone Inheritance

Skin tone is determined by melanin levels, controlled by multiple genes. Both parents contribute to the offspring's skin tone.
